Hawa Mahal, Jaipur, Rajasthan


Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, Hawa Mahal is a famous attraction which finds a place in every Golden Triangle tour in India. It stands at the intersection of the main road called Badi Chaupad.
Constructed of red and pink sandstone, it is widely popular because of its unique style of construction, and is often known as the signature building. An interesting fact about Hawa Mahal is that there are 953 windows, which are called ‘Jharokhas’.
